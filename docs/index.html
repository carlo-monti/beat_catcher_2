BEAT CATCHER

A portable beat-tracking device


WHAT IT IS

Beat Catcher is a beat-tracking device for synchronizing electronic systems and human musicians. Using one sensor on the kick and another on the snare, Beat Catcher detects the tempo currently held by the drummer and sends out a MIDI CLOCK signal that keeps the electronic instruments in sync with it.

WHAT IT DOES

Beat Catcher acts as a MIDI master clock for other MIDI sync-able gear (synths, sequencers, effects, ...). However, unlike other master clocks, it stays in sync with the drummer. It means that if the drummer slows down, it will slow down too!

Let's see a usage example. Let's suppose that your song starts with a drum groove and, after that, a synth sequence comes in (i.e. A-ha - Take On Me).

If you want to play the song live, the drummer must have an in-ear monitor with a metronome, otherwise the drum beat may fail to match the exact bpm of the synth! With Beat Catcher there is no need of a metronome. The drummer can start the whole system (by simply tapping on it) and the synth will receive a MIDI Clock signal that is in synch.

Everything that is connected as slave to the MIDI Clock will be perfectly on time. It could be a sequencer, an arpeggiator, a looper or even a time-based effect (like a delay or a modulator).

HOW IT WORKS

The setup is very simple. First, you have to attach the two sensors (that are similar to Roland RT-30) on the kick and on the snare. After that, you place Beat Catcher where you like it and connect the two wires coming from the sensors to it. Finally you connect your MIDI equipment to it using a standard MIDI cable. The system is ready! You can start it by tapping a 4/4 on it with a drumstick (alternatively you can select the exact bpm and manually trigger the start of the sequence). The system will start the MIDI Clock and you can start playing.

Remember that Beat Catcher can follow your tempo only when you are using kick and snare! If it don't detect any hit, it will keep up with the last bpm detected. You can then follow the tempo of the MIDI Clock by connecting earphones to it or by looking at the four leds that are on top of it (that indicates the 1/4 notes).

HOW TO USE

This is Beat Catcher:

SETUP PHASE

First you have to connect the kick and snare sensors to the two TRS inputs that are on the left side. Then you need to calibrate the input. To do so you have to use the potentiometers to adjust the gain. To do so:

  • Turn on the device by pressing PWR BTN
  • Click on the MODE BTN to enter SETTINGS MODE.
  • Hit strong your kick drum and increase/decrease the gain until the KICK LED barely lits.
  • Hit strong your snare drum and increase/decrease the gain until the SNARE LED barely lits.

Now you have to dive into the menu options to tweak the parameters:

  • Press again the ENCODER to go ahead in the parameters of the menu until you find the option labelled as KICK.
  • Adjust the threshold (rotating the ENCODER)
  • Press again the ENCODER to go ahead in the parameters
  • Adjust the retrigger gate

Do the same for the snare. At the end you will be asked to save values. Press yes if you don't want to do all of the above steps next time you power up the device!

PLAY PHASE

Now you are ready to play! To do so press again MODE BTN to return in PLAY MODE. Now the device is ready. To start the sequence you have two options:

  • Tap 4 times on the pad at the desidered bpm. Beat Catcher will start the sequence in sync with your tapping.
  • Press the ENCODER and rotate it to set the desidered bpm. To start the sequence, press again the ENCODER.

Now the system is up and running! If you start playing on time with it, it will then follow you.
